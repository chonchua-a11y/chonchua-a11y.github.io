Three alternatives checked, and closed

Churn / dormancy recovery

no volume

Only 2 churned and 2 dormant users out of 186 total. Nothing to recover here right now.

New_user activation funnel

already healthy

95.8% of registered users log a first activity within 30 days, median time to first log is 10h 44m. new_user's low total is a tenure artifact, not a stalled funnel.

onboarding_completed as an activation signal

dead telemetry

Only 4 events fired in 12 months against 100+ registrations in the last 90 days alone, no custom properties, and no persona value attached on any of the 4. Not wired into the real signup flow — flagged separately, not used here.

What's left: weekend_warrior and casual_runner are both highly retained — most show up in nearly every week of the 90-day window — but log far less per active week than power_user. That's 98 of 186 users, 53% of the base:

Spec

Proposed fix

A quick-add flow for logging a second activity in one session

Right after a user saves an activity, prompt: "log another activity from today?" No new integration, no wearable sync — just remove the friction of opening the log flow twice. Target weekend_warrior and casual_runner specifically.

  • Backed by a confirmed, sizeable gap — 98 users, 53% of the base.
  • Shippable in a week. A passive-capture integration (auto-detect from a connected wearable) targets the same problem but is a much bigger lift.
  • Churn and activation, the two obvious alternative bets, both checked out fine — no need to build fixes for problems that don't exist.

Reacting to

Open question

The spec assumes weekend_warrior and casual_runner are doing more activity than they log, and that a quick-add nudge would close that gap. We haven't actually confirmed that assumption, we only know they show up often and log little each time. Before investing design time in the flow, how would you validate whether these users have more to log, versus this simply being their real activity ceiling?

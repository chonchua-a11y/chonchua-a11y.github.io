Persona predicts training volume. Sport barely moves it.

Why power users log 5x more than new users over 90 days, what that has to do with primary sport (not much), and what to build about it next week.

Recommendation for next week Build a quick-add flow for logging a second activity in one session Target weekend_warrior and casual_runner — 98 users, 53% of the base, retained but under-logging.
01Hypothesis

What we set out to check

The suspected problem: average activity_created events per user over 90 days ranged from ~15 (new_user) to ~79 (power_user) — a 5x spread across personas.

The claim was that persona predicts activity level more strongly than primary_sport: power_user and triathlete log 2.5–4x more than casual_runner or weekend_warrior in the same sport, and weekend_warrior stays flat around ~20 events regardless of whether the sport is Run, Hike, or Ride.

Signal 3 — The gap is volume per active week, not retention

Weekly stickiness over the 90-day window shows power_user and weekend_warrior are both highly retained — most people in each group show up in nearly every week. Weekend_warrior's low total isn't churn. They're present as often as power_user, they just log 1.6 activities in a typical active week against power_user's 5.9. The fix isn't "get them to come back," it's "get them to log more while they're already here."

03Ruled out

What we checked and set aside

Before committing to a recommendation, three alternative leads were checked against the data. All three closed.

Churn / dormancy recovery

no volume

Only 2 churned and 2 dormant users out of 186 total. Nothing to recover here right now.

New_user activation funnel

already healthy

Looked promising on paper — 48 users, lowest average activity of any persona. But 95.8% of registered users log a first activity within 30 days, median time to first log is 10h 44m. The low total is a tenure artifact, not a stalled funnel.

onboarding_completed as an activation signal

dead telemetry

Only 4 events fired in 12 months against 100+ registrations in the last 90 days alone. No custom properties, and all 4 fired with no persona value attached — while persona is populated on nearly every activity_created event. This event isn't wired into the real signup flow.

04Recommendation

What to do next week

Of the 186 users in the base, weekend_warrior and casual_runner together are 98 of them:

98 users · 53% of the base · retained, under-logging

casual_runner (66) + weekend_warrior (32) new_user (48) · power_user (15) · ultra_runner (8) · competitive_cyclist (7) · triathlete (6)

Build this

A quick-add flow for logging a second activity in the same session

Right after a user saves an activity, prompt: "log another activity from today?" No new integration, no wearable sync — just remove the friction of opening the log flow twice. Target weekend_warrior and casual_runner specifically.

  • Backed by a confirmed, sizeable gap — 98 users, 53% of the base.
  • Shippable in a week. A passive-capture integration (auto-detect from a connected wearable) targets the same problem but is a much bigger lift — worth keeping on the roadmap if this validates that users have more to log than they bother to.
  • Churn and activation, the two obvious alternative bets, both checked out fine. No need to build fixes for problems that don't exist.

Also do — same week, low effort

Retire or properly re-instrument onboarding_completed. It's currently a landmine in the analytics — anyone who builds a dashboard or alert off it will draw false conclusions, the way this investigation almost did.
